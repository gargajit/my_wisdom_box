far behind every feeling and experience. there is the thought. Absence of thoughts, however, does not lead to absence of feelings. On the contrary, a still mind is like the tranquil lake, it's like the calm ocean, an absolute beauty. For thoughts are teh ripples in that lake, emotions are the waves in the oceans of mind. They remain inseparable though - waves from ocean. Good meditators, or even mindful perople, transcend their thoughts by simply watching them. Most thoughts are random and fickle; they are not worth pursuing. Upon close exampination, you will discover that thoughts are highly ephemeral and if you don't hold on to them, they disappear exactly like bubbles in water. Whenever you are bothered by any lingering thought, simply ask yourself the following three questions and watch it become feeble in no time:

  • 1. From where has this thought originated?
  • 2. Where is it traveling?
  • Where has it disappeared?
As you pnder on these, you begin to understand tha anatomy of a thought; basically, its emptiness.
